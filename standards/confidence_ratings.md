# Confidence Ratings

Use these labels consistently in summaries and structured outputs.

## Ratings

- `high`: directly supported by clear source material with little ambiguity
- `medium`: likely supported by source material but includes interpretation or partial ambiguity
- `low`: tentative, inferred, or dependent on missing context

## Usage

- Apply confidence at the finding level when possible.
- Explain why confidence is reduced when assigning `medium` or `low`.
- Do not represent inferred conclusions as factual without a confidence label.
